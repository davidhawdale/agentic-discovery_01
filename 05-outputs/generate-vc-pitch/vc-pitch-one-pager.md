# Personal AI Assistant: Research-Backed VC Pitch

## The Problem

Today's AI tools are capable but not personal. Users re-explain context every session, receive hallucinated results with no warning, and face disconnected apps that force them to be their own integration layer. The core frustration across 53 interviews: AI doesn't remember you, doesn't act until prompted, and can't signal when it's wrong. "There's also no way to know which parts are not accurate, especially if you're not an expert in the field." It presents errors "in a very confident and convincing way, even when it's completely wrong." (Participants 6, 8)

## What Users Actually Want

- **Memory that persists across sessions.** Users re-explain preferences from scratch every time. "I find that when I ask it questions ... remind me. Do you get paid biweekly or whatever? And I think, okay, great. I'm going to have to do this exercise all over again." (Participants 47, 5, 31, ES5)

- **Proactive action, not passive response.** The most resonant vision: an assistant that initiates. "For me, a perfect personal assistant would be ... someone who would take initiatives, more than an assistant who would wait for me to tell it to do things." (Participants 37, FR37, 50, 42)

- **Integration with systems people already live in.** Calendar, email, messaging — as connective tissue. "All of those things live in separate applications ... I end up being my own single point of failure." (Participants 50, 20, 25)

- **Confidence signals on accuracy.** "Does the AI have an awareness of its own level of credibility? Can it give you a rating ... I am X percent confident?" (Participant 45)

- **Human-review before consequential actions.** "I would prefer to approve items before they automatically go to email or send a calendar invite." (Participants 3, 46, 35)

## The Opportunity

- **No tool owns cross-session personal memory.** ChatGPT's memory feature is shallow; Claude and Gemini start fresh by default.
- **Proactive agentic action is untouched.** Every major tool waits to be asked. ChatGPT's operator features are "quite buggy, and you still really need to watch it." (Participant 9)
- **Email and calendar integration is fragmented.** Google and Apple sit on the data; no one has built the reasoning layer above it.
- **Trust transparency is unaddressed.** No consumer AI today signals its own confidence level or cites sources by default.

## What "Trustworthy" Means

- **Honest uncertainty over silent confidence.** Categorical signals ("I'm not confident here — verify this") are what users want. (Participants 45, 6, 8)
- **Consent-based data access.** Users will share data — but only with clear terms, granular controls, and assurance it isn't resold. (Participants 50, 13, PL13, 45)
- **Human-in-the-loop for consequential actions.** Draft the email; let the user send it. (Participants 3, 18, 46)
- **Step-visible reasoning.** "If they can show me the things that they are doing step by step, I can spot whether there's any step that's slightly off track." (Participant 18)

## Our Positioning

Every major AI assistant is a reactive chat interface. None know you across sessions, watch your calendar, or act without being asked. Our assistant wins by doing three things no competitor does: persisting personal context over time, taking proactive action, and signalling honestly when it's uncertain. Memory, initiative, and calibrated trust — that is the gap 53 interviews identified between what AI is today and what users actually need.
