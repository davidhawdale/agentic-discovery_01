## Opportunity Statement

### Revenue Model Options

- **Monthly subscription:** Low commitment barrier, fast churn signal — but higher churn risk before stickiness is proven.
- **Annual subscription:** Higher LTV and lower churn — but asks for commitment before memory value has been demonstrated.
- **Freemium + paid tier:** Lowers acquisition friction — but delays revenue and risks commoditising free-tier features.
- **Per-seat team pricing:** Upside if work-calendar integration resonates — but adds sales complexity before product-market fit is confirmed.

**Recommendation:** Monthly subscription at launch because it yields the fastest willingness-to-pay and churn signals without requiring an annual commitment before persistent memory and proactive-action differentiation has been demonstrated.

### Pricing

A price of $17/month is defensible: it matches mainstream AI chat pricing (ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, Perplexity Pro all at $20) while undercutting marginally to reduce switching friction. Mem.ai ($14.99) and Reclaim.ai ($10–20) anchor the productivity sub-segment below $20. The premium features — durable cross-session memory, proactive action, consent-gated integration — justify matching that ceiling rather than discounting to productivity-tool levels.

### Pricing Experiment

**Hypothesis:** Users who experience a memory recall moment in their first two sessions convert at higher rates, making persistent memory — not proactive action — the primary willingness-to-pay driver at MVP stage.
**Build:** 14-day free trial with full features; paywall at day 15.
**Measure:** Trial-to-paid conversion rate, segmented by memory recall event in sessions 1–2.
**Success signal:** Conversion rate 15% or above among users with a memory recall event.
**Failure signal:** Conversion rate below 8% across both segments.
