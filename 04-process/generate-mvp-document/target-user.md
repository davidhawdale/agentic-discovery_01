## Target User

### Primary User: Maya Patel — Workflow Optimisers

Maya is a 42-year-old operations lead whose daily friction is exactly what the VC pitch identifies. Participant 50 stated it plainly: "all of those things live in separate applications ... I end up being my own single point of failure." Participant 28 described the ideal as an assistant that taps into email and calendar and answers "is there something that I said I would do that I haven't yet achieved?" — persistent memory and proactive action in a single sentence. The Workflow Optimisers archetype has 13 participants, the largest evidence base in the study, and maps onto a reachable segment: productivity-heavy professionals already using calendar and email tools. Their success criteria are concrete, so experiments yield a clear signal.

## Scope

### In Scope

- Persistent cross-session memory of the user's commitments and priorities
- Proactive surfacing of overdue or at-risk commitments before the user asks
- Email and calendar read access to identify tasks, deadlines, and scheduling gaps
- Daily briefing: "What do I have today? What did I say I would do that I haven't done?"
- Human-review step before any outbound action — user approves before send

### Out of Scope

- Autonomous outbound action without approval — trust must be earned first (Participants 3, 46, 35)
- Creative generation, emotional support, DIY guidance — deferred to preserve focus
- Confidence signalling — relevant for Guarded Verifiers but adds scope risk to v1
- Team-level coordination — dilutes the single-user learning signal
- Integrations beyond email and calendar — extend after core loop is validated

## User Validation Experiment

**Hypothesis:** Workflow Optimisers who connect email and calendar to a persistent-memory assistant will use daily briefing and commitment-tracking at least three times per week, because it removes the overhead they currently absorb themselves.

**Build:** A daily briefing interface reading Gmail and Google Calendar. The assistant maintains a running commitment list and surfaces a prioritized morning briefing. All outbound actions require user approval.

**Measure:** Weekly active use rate of daily briefing across 20 Workflow Optimiser-profile users over a 4-week pilot.

**Success signal:** 60% open the briefing at least 3 times per week by week 3; 40% describe commitment-tracking as "would miss it if removed."

**Failure signal:** Fewer than 30% open the briefing more than once per week after week 1, or output requires too much correction to be useful.
